Bird flu (avian influenza) is spreading rapidly across Kathmandu Valley, affecting poultry farms and wild bird populations. Health authorities have confirmed multiple outbreaks in commercial poultry operations across Bhaktapur, Lalitpur, and Kathmandu districts. The Department of Livestock Services has ordered culling of thousands of birds in affected zones and established quarantine perimeters. Public health officials are monitoring poultry workers for symptoms while emphasizing that transmission to humans remains rare. The outbreak comes during peak summer when wild migratory patterns and high-density urban poultry farming create conditions for rapid viral spread.
